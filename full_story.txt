In the bustling streets of New York City (1950s), Protagonist, a young jazz saxophonist named Miles, practices late into the night. His raw, passionate playing disturbs his elderly neighbor, Mrs. Jenkins, who complains to the landlord. Fearful of being evicted, Miles's goal is to find a quieter place to practice. However, the central conflict arises when he discovers an old, abandoned jazz club nearby. It's perfect for him, but it's also a mystery how he'll afford the rent.
